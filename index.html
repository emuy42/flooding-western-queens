Population growth in a western Queens neighborhood strains local infrastructure, causing flooding

During New York's last major storm, residents of Community Board 2 – which includes Sunnyside, Woodside, and parts of Long Island City – reported significant flooding. Residents blame the sewers.

By Elisa Muyl

Nov. 30, 2023

As rain poured on Sept. 29, residents of Sunnyside, Woodside, and southern Long Island City submitted the most 311 complaints per capita about inland flooding in Queens.

The district ranked ahead of Community District 13 – which includes Springfield Gardens and Rosedale, north of JFK airport – and Community District 14, Rockaway. Flooding in southern Queens, an area hit hard during Superstorm Sandy, is an established issue.

In western Queens, the problem is more recent. Residents blame an aging sewer system, which they say isn’t equipped to handle the district’s population. Long Island City’s population has grown significantly since it was rezoned in 2001, causing a population boom in Sunnyside and Woodside further east.

“It’s not just sewers that are crumbling,” said Danielle Brecker, chair of Community Board 2. “There’s more people, they’re taxing the infrastructure, and it’s crumbling.”
